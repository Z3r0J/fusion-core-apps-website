## What Is a Warranty Scanner?

A **warranty scanner** is an app that uses your phone's camera to capture purchase receipts, automatically extracts the key details — product, store, purchase date, price — and turns them into tracked warranties with countdown timers and expiry alerts. Instead of a shoebox of fading paper, you get a searchable inventory of everything you own and exactly how long each warranty has left.

If you have ever discovered a broken appliance was still under warranty *two weeks after* the coverage ended, this category of app exists for you.

## Why Receipts Are Terrible Warranty Proof

The receipt is usually the only proof of purchase a retailer or manufacturer will accept — and it is the single worst-designed document for the job:

- **Thermal paper fades.** Many receipts are illegible within a year, often before the warranty ends.
- **They get lost.** Wallets, junk drawers, glove compartments — nobody has a system.
- **They tell you nothing.** A receipt does not know when your coverage expires; you would have to track that separately.

Warranties themselves make it worse: a single product can carry a store warranty, a manufacturer warranty, and an extended warranty — each with a different clock.

## How Warranty Scanner Apps Work (OCR + AI)

Modern warranty scanners combine the camera with AI text extraction:

1. **Scan** — point your camera at the receipt.
2. **Extract** — AI reads the receipt and auto-fills the product name, store, purchase date, and price. No manual typing.
3. **Track** — you confirm the warranty length (or lengths), and the app starts a countdown per item.
4. **Alert** — before a warranty expires, you get a notification with enough lead time to actually file a claim.

The scan also preserves a permanent, legible copy of the receipt — which is what you will actually hand to customer service when you claim.

## What to Look For in a Warranty Tracker

Not every receipt app tracks warranties, and not every warranty app scans receipts. The useful combination:

- **AI receipt scanning** — if you have to type in every purchase manually, you will stop after three items.
- **Multiple warranty types per product** — store, manufacturer, and extended coverage tracked separately.
- **Expiry alerts** — the entire point. A tracker without proactive notifications is just a list.
- **Attachments** — invoices, warranty certificates, and product photos kept with each item.
- **A priority view** — what is expiring soon, at a glance.

## How to File a Warranty Claim Before the Deadline

When something breaks, the process is simpler than most people expect — *if* you have the paperwork:

1. **Check the coverage.** Find the purchase date and the warranty length. A tracker app shows this instantly.
2. **Gather proof of purchase.** The scanned receipt, plus any warranty certificate.
3. **Contact the right party.** Store warranty → the retailer. Manufacturer warranty → the brand's support line or site.
4. **Describe the defect factually.** When it started, what happens, what you have tried.
5. **File before the expiry date.** Claims submitted even one day late are routinely denied — this is where expiry alerts earn their keep.
