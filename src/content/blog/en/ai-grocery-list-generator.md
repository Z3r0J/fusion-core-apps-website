## What an AI Grocery List Generator Does

Instead of typing your shopping list item by item, you just say what you need — and the app builds the list for you. That's the whole idea. You talk the way you normally would ("two liters of milk, a dozen eggs, some bananas…") and the items show up on your list, already separated and sorted.

The point isn't novelty. It's speed. Rattling off a week's shopping out loud takes a few seconds. Typing it takes minutes — and you usually forget something halfway through.

## Build Your List Just by Talking

In [CartWise](/cartwise), this takes four quick steps:

**Step 1 — Tap the microphone on your list**
It's in the list's action bar. Tap it and start speaking.

**Step 2 — Say everything you need, naturally**
No special phrasing. Just talk: "two liters of milk, a dozen eggs, chicken thighs, dish soap, and some bananas." It listens in your language — you don't switch to English.

**Step 3 — Check the list it builds**
CartWise turns your words into separate items, each with a quantity, and groups them by store section. You see the full list before anything is added — keep what you want, drop what you don't, or record again if you misspoke.

**Step 4 — Done, and ready to shop**
Confirm, and the items land on your list, already sorted by aisle so you move through the store in order. They're saved on your phone, so the list still works even with no signal.

## It Stays in Your Language

Say it in Spanish, Portuguese, German, or English, and your items stay in that language — "leche" stays "leche," not "milk." If you shop in a language other than English, that's the difference between a list you can read at a glance and one you have to re-translate in the aisle.

## Nothing Gets Added Without Your Okay

The AI never writes straight to your list. It shows you what it understood first, and you confirm. So if it mishears something, you catch it in two seconds — your list never fills up with surprises.

## Don't Feel Like Talking? Start From a Ready-Made List

The starters rotate over time, so they stay fresh and you're not looking at the same suggestions every week.
