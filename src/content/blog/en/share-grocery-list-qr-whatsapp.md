## Texting Items Back and Forth Gets Messy

You're heading to the store and message your partner: "Anything we need?" They send three items. Halfway down the aisles, two more arrive. By checkout you've got a thread to piece together, duplicates to sort out, and the feeling you still forgot something.

CartWise gives you two cleaner ways to share — but they're not the same thing, and picking the right one matters.

## Two Ways to Share — and They're Different

- **QR code or invite code → shop together.** This turns your list into a *live shared list*. Everyone who joins can add and check off items, and every change shows up on all phones in real time.
- **WhatsApp → send a copy.** This sends a neat, formatted *snapshot* of your list as a message. The other person can read it, but they can't edit it and it won't update.

Rule of thumb: if you're both going to keep changing the list, share the live one. If someone just needs to *see* it, send a copy.

## Share a Live List (QR Code or Invite Code)

Best when you're shopping together or keeping a household list everyone adds to.

**The owner starts it.** Open the list and tap **Share** (only the list's owner can start a shared list). CartWise shows two things:

- a **QR code** to scan, and
- a **6-character invite code** for reading aloud — it leaves out look-alike characters like O/0 and I/1, so it's easy to say over the phone without confusion.

**The other person can join in three ways:**

1. **Scan inside CartWise** — they open the app, tap Join, and scan the code.
2. **Scan with any phone camera** — point the regular camera app at the QR and CartWise opens straight to the join screen.
3. **Type the code** — enter the 6 characters by hand.

Once they're in, the list is live for everyone: add an item, check one off, or remove one, and it appears on every phone within seconds. It works offline too — changes you make with no signal catch up automatically when you're back online — and members get a notification when someone adds something.

The invite code lasts **7 days**. If you ever want to cut off access or hand out a fresh one, the owner can regenerate it from the share screen.

## Send a Copy via WhatsApp (or Anywhere)

Best when someone just needs to see the list and won't be editing it.

1. Open the list and tap the **⋮ menu**.
2. Choose **Share as text**. CartWise builds a clean, formatted message — grouped by section, with checkboxes and quantities:

```
🛒 Weekly Groceries

---- Dairy ----
  ☐ Milk - 2 L
  ☑ Eggs - 12

---- Fruit & Veg ----
  ☐ Bananas - 1 kg

📊 Total: 3 items
✅ Done: 1/3

Shared via CartWise
```

3. Pick **WhatsApp** from the share sheet (Messages, Telegram, and email work too), choose a chat, and the list is already filled in — just send.

In the same menu there's also **Share as image**, which sends a styled card instead of text — handy for a WhatsApp Status or an Instagram story.

Just remember: this is a snapshot. It won't update, and the other person can't edit it. For a list you both keep changing, use the QR or invite code above.

## Which One Should You Use?

- **Shopping together or a shared household list →** QR code / invite code. A living list everyone updates in real time.
- **Just sending it to someone →** WhatsApp text or image. A quick, read-only copy.

## No Account Needed — for Either Person

Nobody has to sign up. Neither the owner nor the person joining needs an account or a login. The live list keeps working even with bad signal in the store and syncs back up the moment you're online again — and if a friend has never used CartWise, they can install it, scan the code, and be on the shared list in under a minute.
