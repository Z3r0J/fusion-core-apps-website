## Budgeting and price tracking

CartWise is built around a live price calculator: assign a price to an item when you add it, and the app keeps a running cart total as you check things off. You know what you owe before you reach the register. The reports screen goes further, logging spend per trip so you can spot budget patterns week over week.

OurGroceries has never offered price tracking in any form. There is no field for item costs, no cart total, and no spending history. It is a list tool, not a financial one — and after more than a decade of user requests, that gap shows no sign of closing.

For households trying to manage a weekly grocery budget, this is not a minor difference. CartWise has a structural advantage here that OurGroceries cannot close with a settings toggle.

## Sharing and accounts

OurGroceries requires an account to share a list. Once both parties are signed in, the sync is fast and reliable — changes appear on the other device in real time, and the experience is genuinely frictionless once set up. The account requirement is the friction.

[CartWise](/cartwise) skips the account entirely. Share a list by showing someone a QR code or sending a WhatsApp link. The recipient joins without creating a login. Real-time sync works the same way on both ends. For sending a list to a parent, a partner, or anyone who resists signing up for yet another service, that zero-friction model is a meaningful practical advantage.

The trade-off is that OurGroceries' sharing model is more mature and battle-tested across a wider range of devices. If you and your household members are already in the OurGroceries ecosystem, the account step is a one-time cost you have already paid.

## Voice and AI

Both apps support voice input for hands-free item entry. OurGroceries goes further on voice-assistant integration: you can add items via Alexa and Siri, which is genuinely useful if you have a smart speaker in the kitchen and want to call out items mid-cooking without touching your phone.

CartWise counters with AI list generation. Describe a meal, a dinner-party theme, or a recipe idea, and the app drafts the full ingredient list. OurGroceries' AI feature is narrower — it can scan a photo and extract items from it, but it does not generate lists from a natural-language prompt.

The honest summary: OurGroceries has better voice-assistant breadth; CartWise has more capable AI. If you live in an Alexa household, OurGroceries' edge is real. If you want to go from zero to a complete list in a single prompt, CartWise is the better tool.

## Platforms and reliability

OurGroceries' biggest practical advantage is coverage. It runs on iOS, Android, the web, Apple Watch, and Wear OS. If your household mixes iPhones and Android phones — or if you want to glance at your list from a smartwatch — OurGroceries is the only option between the two. That breadth, combined with years of refinement, gives it a reliability track record CartWise is still building.

CartWise is Android-only. That is a real constraint for cross-platform households and worth naming plainly. What CartWise trades for narrower reach is a more modern, actively updated interface — and support for 8 languages versus OurGroceries' more limited localization. If your household is Android-first and willing to trade proven longevity for a cleaner UX and richer features, CartWise is the stronger day-to-day experience.
