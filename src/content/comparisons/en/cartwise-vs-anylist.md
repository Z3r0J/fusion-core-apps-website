## Price tracking: free vs paywalled

CartWise puts a live price calculator at the center of the shopping experience. Add a price when you put something on the list, and a running total updates as you check items off. By the time you reach the register you already know roughly what you owe. The reports screen extends this further, logging spend per trip so you can spot where your grocery budget actually goes each month. All of this is free, with no upgrade required.

AnyList supports item prices, but that feature sits behind the paid AnyList Complete plan ($9.99–14.99/year). On the free tier, there is no way to add prices or track what a shopping trip costs. That is not a knock against AnyList — it is simply not a budgeting tool at its core — but it is a meaningful distinction if spending visibility is important to you.

For households that want one app to cover both the list and the budget, CartWise has a structural advantage that does not depend on a subscription.

## Recipes and meal planning

This is AnyList's clearest strength, and it is worth saying plainly: if recipe management and meal planning matter to you, AnyList is genuinely excellent at both. You can import recipes from thousands of sites with a single tap, scale servings up or down, and use a dedicated cook mode while you work at the stove. A full meal-planning calendar lets you schedule the week's dinners, sync with Google Calendar, and push every ingredient onto your grocery list automatically.

CartWise has no equivalent. It is built around the shopping trip itself — adding items fast, tracking the total, and sharing the list — not around what happens before you set foot in the store. If you regularly cook from recipes and plan meals weekly, that gap is real and CartWise is not the right tool.

The honest summary: AnyList wins this category by a wide margin. Choose it if cooking and meal planning are central to how you use a grocery app.

## Sharing and getting started

CartWise makes sharing as low-friction as possible. Generate a QR code, send a WhatsApp link, or share via any messaging app — the person you invite does not need an account to join the list. For a family member who would never bother creating another login, that zero-friction entry is a genuine advantage. Voice input and AI list generation mean you can go from zero to a complete list without typing a word.

AnyList also supports real-time collaborative lists, but sharing works through account-based email invites. Both people need an AnyList account for full collaboration, which adds a registration step before you get any shared-list value. There is no QR code or WhatsApp-style link sharing.

If you are sending a list to someone who is not already on AnyList, the onboarding overhead is worth factoring in. CartWise removes that step entirely.

## Platforms, languages, and ads

AnyList wins on platform breadth. It runs on iPhone, iPad, Mac, the web, and Apple Watch — and the iOS experience in particular is polished and fast. If your household mixes iPhones and Android phones, AnyList is the only viable option between these two. It is also completely ad-free across all apps, even on the free tier, which is a genuine quality-of-life difference for everyday use.

[CartWise](/cartwise) is Android-only at this stage, which is a real limitation for mixed-platform households and worth naming plainly. Where CartWise pulls ahead is language support: the app is available in 8 languages — Spanish, French, German, Italian, Portuguese, Dutch, Polish, and English — while AnyList is English-only. For non-English-speaking households or multilingual families, that gap is significant.

The trade-off is clear: AnyList gives you more platforms and a fully ad-free experience; CartWise gives you broader language support and free price tracking. Pick by what your household actually needs most.
